## TURBOTENANT — DETAILED FINDINGS

### What Users Love
- All-in-one: listings auto-post to multiple platforms, screening, leases, payments
- State-specific lease templates — legally compliant
- "Dumbed down the interface to the point that it is near fun" (Raghu Giuffre, Nov 2025)
- Free base tier — huge adoption driver for small landlords
- E-signature built in
- Good for 1–15 unit self-managing landlords
- Phone support available (rare in this category)

### What Users Hate
- Payment glitches — tenant payment blocked for 10 days with no explanation (Gary, Jun 2025)
- Support hours too limited — "outside support hours" at 3:30pm PST (customer, May 2025)
- Azibo acquisition migration — users moved over without clear communication, free trial expired
  without notification (AC, Jan 2026)
- Chat bot useless for real problems
- Lease renewal edge case: changing one tenant's email breaks the whole renewal flow
- Application fees charged to tenants even when property already rented — no refund
  (Dionna, Nov 2025)
- Pricing creep — "more invasive with new options" as platform matures (Kjb, Jan 2026)

### The Gap TurboTenant Does NOT Fill
- Zero investor analytics (DSCR, cash-on-cash, equity growth, 30-year projection)
- No BRRRR tracking
- No HELOC/credit line tracking
- No entity/LLC awareness
- No portfolio-level views
- Designed for beginner landlords — not active investors scaling a portfolio

## DOORLOOP — DETAILED FINDINGS

### What Users Love
- Best onboarding in category — "seamless," "walked through everything"
- Modern UI — most visually polished platform
- Responsive customer service (when it works)
- Work order / maintenance tracking is solid
- Good for property management companies (professional PM, not DIY investors)

### What Users Hate (1-star reviews — GOLD for our research)
- **Hidden fees on tenants:**
  - $0.99/mo ACH fee (Warren Cottingham, Feb 2026): "I don't know of any other company who charges anything for ACH"
  - 3.25% credit card fee every month
  - $2 fee even when using Plaid bank link (Ian Bryan, Mar 2025)
  - Tenants threatening to move out over fees
- **Payment processing problems:**
  - 3–5 day hold after money leaves tenant's bank (Jose Rivera, Jun 2025)
  - NSF charges hit the OWNER's account instead of the tenant (Glo Up, Aug 2024)
  - Lease rollover glitch: if you don't manually renew at expiration, system STOPS charging tenant
    automatically — even if tenant hasn't moved out (Glo Up, Aug 2024)
- **Support is chat-only:**
  - No real phone number — "call number just hangs up and texts you a link to the website" (Ben Tally, Apr 2025)
  - Wait times: "59 minutes waiting for a teammate" (Anon, Sep 2025)
  - AI bot "did the absolute minimum effort" to understand the issue
  - Sales team overpromised support — "they certainly got me suckered into this" (Anon, Sep 2025)
- **QuickBooks integration destroyed data** — "lost everything" (Zebby Trota, Jun 2025)
- **Counter-intuitive UI for complex tasks:** "unnecessarily complex for simple tasks like
  managing leases and tenants" (Mark Neuman, Apr 2025)
- **Pricing locked in annually — no refunds:** customer paid $1,000+, barely used it,
  no refund given (Ava Ighanian, Jun 2024)
- **Training focused only on tenant payments**, not investor/owner workflows

### The Gap DoorLoop Does NOT Fill
- Same as TurboTenant — zero investor analytics
- Built for professional property management companies, not DIY investors
- No BRRRR, no HELOC tracking, no LLC structure awareness
- Pricing is enterprise-grade ($59–$239/mo) — prohibitive for a growing portfolio

## UNIVERSAL PAIN POINTS (across all platforms)

1. **Pricing creep** — starts free/cheap, balloons as portfolio grows
2. **Hidden fees passed to tenants** — damages landlord-tenant relationship
3. **Chat-only support with bots** — industry-wide failure
4. **No investor analytics** — DSCR, equity, 30-year projections, cap rate trends
5. **No BRRRR/capital recycling tracking** — nobody does this
6. **No LLC/entity structure awareness** — tools don't understand Series LLC, trust structures
7. **Payment glitches** — rent not processing, NSF charged to wrong party, lease rollover bugs
8. **QuickBooks/integration failures** — data loss when connecting third-party accounting
9. **Overpromised by sales, underdelivered by product** — DoorLoop complaint pattern
10. **Feature bloat** — tools stuffed with features landlords never use
11. **Mobile apps are browser wrappers** — not true native apps
12. **Annual contracts with no refunds** — lock-in hostility

## STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS FOR OUR BUILD

1. **Core 4 must be BULLETPROOF first:** rent tracking, expense logging, tenant records,
   document storage. Everything else is secondary. Landlords abandon tools that glitch here.

2. **Never charge tenants fees.** DoorLoop's single biggest 1-star driver. Our app is
   landlord-side only. Tenants pay nothing through our platform.

3. **No chat bots for support.** We're a personal tool — the "support" IS the app itself.
   Local-first means no account needed, no server to call, no support queue.

4. **Lease rollover must be explicit, never silent.** DoorLoop's bug of silently stopping
   charges at lease end is catastrophic. Our alert system (15.1.2) covers this.

5. **The investor analytics gap is REAL and WIDE.** Nobody in this market does DSCR tracking,
   BRRRR cycle management, or 30-year projections. This is our moat. Do not deprioritize
   Sections 9.2–9.3 of the spec.

6. **Innago is the free competitor to watch.** 4.7 rating, free, clean. We need to be
   meaningfully better — not just free, but investor-smart.

7. **Baselane's banking angle is interesting.** Not a threat to us — different tool. But their
   "track by property" approach validates our per-property ledger design.
