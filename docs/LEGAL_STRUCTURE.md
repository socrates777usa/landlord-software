## 1. LLC Formation — State, Structure, Registered Agent

### 1.1 State of Formation: TEXAS (decided)
- Business-friendly, low regulatory burden
- No state income tax
- Strong Series LLC laws (available since 2009)
- Properties are in Texas — keeps it simple
- Filing fee: $300 with Texas Secretary of State

### 1.2 Structure: Series LLC (recommended)

**Option 1 — Traditional Single LLC**
- Simple, one entity holds all properties
- Pros: Easy setup, one tax return, one bank account
- Cons: If one property gets sued, all properties are at risk

**Option 2 — Series LLC (RECOMMENDED)**
- Parent LLC with individual "series" for each property (or groups)
- Each series insulated from liabilities of other series and the parent
- No additional $300 filing fee per series — just the parent LLC
- Requirements: Series language in Certificate of Formation, separate accounting per series, sign contracts in name of specific series, separate bank accounts recommended
- Limit: Consider 2 parent LLCs at 20+ series (40+ properties)

**Option 3 — Multiple Traditional LLCs**
- Maximum protection, maximum overhead
- $300 per LLC, separate registered agents, separate returns
- Administrative nightmare at scale — not recommended

### 1.3 Registered Agent
- Professional service recommended: ~$100/year
- Keeps home address private, ensures legal notices never missed
- Must be Texas resident or TX-authorized corporation, physical TX address, available business hours
- Options: Northwest, ZenBusiness, others

## 2. Entity Structure for Trust — When/If Needed

### 2.1 When to Add Trust Layer
- Start with LLC only
- Add trust layer if divorce risk emerges (implementable within a couple months)

**Trust Types:**
- **Revocable Living Trust** — you control it, easy to modify, avoids probate, does NOT protect from creditors
- **Irrevocable Trust** — removes assets from estate, creditor protection, harder to modify, better for legacy/succession
- **Recommendation**: Irrevocable trust with LLC membership interests transferred in

### 2.2 Board Composition (When Trust Activated)
- Trustee(s) — manages trust, makes distributions
- Successor Trustee(s) — takes over if primary can't serve
- Trust Protector (optional) — can modify trust, remove trustees
- At least 2-3 trustees, clear succession order, defined powers and limits

**Beneficiary Hierarchy (per Brian's plan):**
- Brian alive → no payout
- Brian dead, Suzanne alive → Suzanne receives
- Both dead → kids receive

## 3. Operating Agreement — Ownership, Decision Rights, Succession

### 3.1 Why You Need This
- Texas does not require one, but default rules are often NOT what you'd want
- Critical for estate planning, succession, and dispute resolution

### 3.2 Key Provisions

**Ownership & Capital**
- Member names and ownership percentages
- Initial capital contributions
- Capital call process
- Process for admitting new members

**Management Structure**
- Manager-managed (not member-managed) — you want control
- What decisions require member vote vs manager authority
- Signing authority for contracts, leases, loans

**Profit & Loss**
- How profits are distributed (or retained during accumulation phase)
- Tax treatment elections
- Minimum distributions to cover tax liabilities — phantom income issue

**Transfer & Exit**
- Right of first refusal on membership interest sales
- Buyout clauses and valuation method
- Restrictions on transfers to third parties

**Succession & Death**
- Named successor manager
- What happens to membership interest at death
- Spouse rights (income beneficiary, voting rights)
- Integration with trust structure

**Dissolution**
- Dissolution triggers
- Property liquidation process
- Unanimous consent vs majority vote

**Dispute Resolution**
- Mediation or arbitration before lawsuit
- Governing law: Texas
- Venue for disputes

**Series LLC Additional Provisions**
- Naming conventions for series
- How new series are created
- Inter-series transactions
- Series-specific bank accounts and records

## 3.3 Annual Compliance
- All Texas LLCs must file Public Information Report + Franchise Tax by May 15
- Franchise tax: 1% of gross receipts over $1 million
- Maintain meeting minutes (even informal notes)
- Separate books/records for each series

## 3.4 Federal Requirement — BOI Report
- Starting 2024: file Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) report with FinCEN
- Free to file
- Must be filed within 90 days of LLC formation
- Update if ownership changes
